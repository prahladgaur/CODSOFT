Jaisalmer Fort

Awarded the title of UNESCO World Heritage Site, the Jaisalmer Fort is a marvel to look at. It was built in 1156 AD and was the first edifice constructed in Jaisalmer. The fort was built by Raja Rawal Jaisal, the founder of Jaisalmer. He found the land where Jaisalmer sits now, made a mud castle on a hilltop and named the city after himself. Jaisalmer literally translates to ‘the hill fort of Jaisal’.

Hawa Mahal

Also known as the Palace of Breeze, the Hawa Mahal is a marvellous monument which provides a glimpse of the culture in 18th century Rajasthan. Being one of the major tourist attractions of the city, the palace is made out of sandstone and features around 953 windows.

Junagarh Fort

Constructed under the commission of Raja Rai Singh in 1588, the Junagarh Fort is an impregnable bastion which is made up of red sandstone and marble. It is one of the primary Bikaner attractions in the city and has attracted people from across the globe.

Gwalior Fort

It is one of the most beautiful edifices in Madhya Pradesh, which is situated on a beautiful hilltop and is considered to be an impenetrable fortress. While archaeologists and historians still haven’t pinpointed the exact date of its foundation, according to the local legends, the Gwalior Fort was constructed by King Suraj Sen in the 3rd century CE. A popular legend amongst the locals suggests that the city got its name from a hermit saint named Gwalipa, who saved Suraj Sen from a deadly disease.

Taj Mahal

One of the seven wonders of the world, the Taj Mahal is a magnificent edifice in the city of Agra, memorialising the love of an emperor for his queen. This amazing monument was commissioned by the Mughal emperor, Shah Jahan, in mid 17th century honouring his deceased wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died in childbirth. This mausoleum of white marble took approximately 22 years to be built. Millions of people from across the globe visit this monument to bask in its beauty.

Agra Fort

Built in 1565, the Agra Fort was the residence of Mughal emperors residing near the Yamuna River in Agra. Commissioned by the great emperor Akbar, the fort is a 70 feet high structure made out of beautiful red sandstone that emulates the Mughal empire’s beautiful architecture. The fort was awarded the title of UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1983.

Red Fort

Also known as the Lal Kila, the Red Fort is one of the most precious and well-preserved legacies of the Mughal empire, which ruled India for centuries. One of the most popular tourist attractions in the capital of the country, it was built by Shah Jahan during the mid-17th century. The 75 ft fort is entirely constructed with red sandstones. It shows the infrastructural Prowse of the architects and engineers during the Mughal empire and their capability of constructing iconic structural beauties.

Mehrangarh Fort

Boasting its marvellous architecture and picturesque landscape, the Mehrangarh Fort is one of many exquisite palaces in Jodhpur. Now constructed into a museum trust, the fort was constructed in the mid-15th century under the orders of Rao Jodha, the founder of Jodhpur. The fort spans over 5 km atop a 125-m high hill which looks over the beautiful city. You can explore the beauty of Jodhpur through a panoramic view from the Mehrangarh Fort, as well as check out the historical artefacts of its time.

Amer Fort

Also called the Amber Palace, Amer Fort is an iconic structure in Jaipur that is endowed with scenic beauty. It is one of the most popular tourist attractions of the city, which sits on a small hill and emulates the essence of Rajput architecture. It was built in the 17th century by Raja Man Singh, and it is entirely constructed out of pale yellow and pink sandstone. Legend has it that the fort also has secret tunnels that lead directly to Jaigarh Fort.

Kangra Fort

Holding a lot of historical and mythical importance of Indian heritage, Kangra Fort is one of the most picturesque forts in the country. One of the oldest and largest forts in India, the fort is believed to date back to the time of Mahabharat and also features in the story. The Katoch Dynasty were the first residents of the fort, who are considered to be the oldest surviving royal dynasty in the world.

Orchha Fort Complex

The Orchha Fort complex, which houses a large number of ancient monuments consisting of the fort, palaces, temple, and other edifices, is located in the Orchha town in the Indian state of Madhya Pradesh. The fort and other structures within it were built by the Bundela Rajputs starting from the early 16th century by King Rudra Pratap Singh of the Orchha State and others who followed him.
